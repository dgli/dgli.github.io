The final summative project of the Structures and Materials course (COV102) during year one was (surprise surprise) to construct a bridge. The bridge was to be constructed out of dense highquality cardboard and contact cement.

Key Features

  • Demonstrates knowledge learned in CIV102
  • Hands on construction
  • high strength to mass ratio

Design Challenges

The role of a bridge is to withstand large loads by making good use of the available materials. Two bridges made with the exact same material may have very different load results due to the difference in design. A good bridge typically have a very high strength to mass ratio, while a poorly designed/made bridge will collapse under very little loads.

  • Use no more than 32" * 40" * 0.05" of cardboard
  • Use no adhesive or joining device but contact cement
  • Must span a 1-meter gap
  • Must be a box-girder bridge (there wasn't really a choice)
  • me be able to resist large bending moment forces.
