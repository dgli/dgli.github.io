In Praxis II, students were placed in groups to solve a problem related to the citizens of the Greater Toronto Area. Our team was given the RFP dealing with RSI (repetitive stress injury) in librarians during the shelving motion.

Key Features

  • Relieves RSI in librarians
  • Easy to use and operate
  • Requires a very reasonable amount of funding
  • Does not require replacement of current carts or shelves

Design Challenges

When addressing the issue of RSI in librarians, it is important to not only prevent RSI, but to do it in such a way that does not obstruct the regular routines of librarians. It wouldn't not require mass replacement of shelves or library carts, and it should it require too much funding to implement.

  • The desigh must be non-obstrusive
  • The design should be easy to adapt to
  • The solution should be reasonable in costs
  • The solution should be modular and flexible
